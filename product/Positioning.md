# Positioning — Chargeback Evidence Pack Builder
**Version:** 1.1 (Sharpened)
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-30

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Primary ICP:**
Solo founder or 2-5 person e-commerce or SaaS team. Processes $15k–$300k/month on Stripe. Has received at least one chargeback in the last 90 days. Has already tried to respond to a dispute — and lost, or felt completely lost doing it. Does not have a lawyer, a risk team, or a chargeback management service.

**Psychographic signal:** They Google "how to write a chargeback rebuttal letter" at 11pm the night before a deadline.

**Secondary ICP:**
Freelancer or digital product seller (courses, templates, subscriptions) who faces recurring "subscription cancelled" or "product not as described" disputes.

**Negative ICP (do not target):**
- Merchants who already use Chargebacks911, Kount, or Signifyd (they have enterprise tooling)
- Merchants who want to dispute on volume (>20/month) without reviewing each pack
- Merchants seeking legal advice or representation

## 2. Pain (What They're Actually Feeling)

1. "I don't know what evidence Visa/Mastercard actually wants for this specific dispute reason."
2. "I wrote a rebuttal letter that felt weak but I submitted it anyway because I had nothing better."
3. "I paid a lawyer $300 to write one letter. I can't afford that for every dispute."
4. "I copied a template I found online and it felt completely wrong for my situation."
5. "I missed the response deadline because I didn't realize how tight it was."
6. "My evidence was all over the place — screenshots, emails, PDFs — and I had no idea what order to put them in."

Core emotional state: **overwhelmed, underprepared, on a deadline, and feeling like the system is rigged against them.**

## 3. Value Proposition

**One-sentence VP:**
Chargeback Evidence Pack Builder gives Stripe merchants a submission-grade, dispute-category-specific evidence pack in 30 minutes — built the way card networks want to see it, not the way you guess they do.

**What it does:**
- Asks you the exact questions that matter for your dispute category
- Tells you exactly what documents to include (and why)
- Writes the rebuttal narrative from your answers
- Assembles everything into a single, properly labeled, submission-ready PDF
- Tracks your deadline so you don't miss it

**What it does not do:**
- Does not promise to win your dispute
- Does not provide legal advice
- Does not file the dispute for you

## 5. Commodity Risk Analysis and Countermeasures

### Risk 1: ChatGPT writes rebuttal letters for free
**Countermeasure:** Our output is not just a letter. It's a complete, labeled, structured PDF with exhibits. GPT outputs text. We output submission packages. Reinforce this in every headline.

### Risk 2: Stripe ships a native dispute helper
**Countermeasure:** Stripe's tools serve Stripe's interests (low dispute rates). We serve the merchant's interest (winning the dispute). We go deeper on evidence assembly than Stripe ever will. Speed to build a deeper evidence matrix first.

### Risk 3: A competitor copies the category checklists
**Countermeasure:** Checklists are table stakes. The moat is the intake questions, the evidence gap detection, and the outcome data over time. Ship gap detection before any competitor catches up on checklists.

### Risk 4: Merchants use a $5 Fiverr gig for rebuttal letters
**Countermeasure:** Speed and repeatability. A Fiverr gig takes 2-3 days. We deliver in 30 minutes. Anchor this in all copy: "Submit before the deadline closes."

### Risk 5: A "chargeback coach" YouTube channel posts free templates
**Countermeasure:** Templates don't generate the pack. We own the generation + formatting step. Make the PDF output quality visually impressive — merchants who see it will not want to go back to a Word doc.

### Risk 6: Chargebacks911 or Midigator launches a self-serve tier
**Countermeasure:** They will always add complexity. We stay ruthlessly simple. One category, one pack, 30 minutes. Never add "dispute analytics" to the core flow in v1.

### Risk 7: Merchants stop bothering to dispute (learned helplessness)
**Countermeasure:** Show win-rate data for merchants who submit strong evidence packs vs. weak ones. Frame every pack as "your best shot, fully loaded." Trust and optimism over resignation.

### Risk 8: AI commoditizes rebuttal narrative writing specifically
**Countermeasure:** The narrative is the least defensible part. Double down on the structured evidence matrix and the PDF assembly. The narrative is a feature; the evidence pack is the product.

### Risk 9: Network evidence rules change and our checklists go stale
**Countermeasure:** Ship a lightweight admin tool to update checklists without a full deploy. Version the evidence matrix. Make update cadence a trust signal ("Updated for 2026 Visa rules").

### Risk 10: Merchant loses anyway and blames the product
**Countermeasure:** Set expectations explicitly at every step. "This pack gives you the strongest possible submission. Card networks make the final decision." Never let the product imply it guarantees outcomes.

## 9. Pricing Psychology

**Framing:** $39 is anchored against a $300–$500 lawyer letter. Always present it this way.

**Bundle offer:** 3 packs for $99 (saves ~$18) creates urgency for merchants who know they'll face future disputes. Positions them as a "recurring need" buyer, not a one-time user.

**No free tier:** A free tier trains merchants to expect free and creates support burden. A free "demo preview" (watermarked, partial) may work as a conversion tool — test post-launch.

**No monthly subscription in v1:** Subscriptions add friction for a tool used episodically. Monthly billing would feel predatory for a merchant who only has one dispute this quarter.

**Price ceiling signal:** The $39 price point should feel like a relief, not a bargain. "Of course it's worth $39. I was going to pay $300."

## 10. Trust Positioning

**What builds trust for this ICP:**
1. **No guaranteed wins language** — ever. Merchants who have been burned by over-promising services will flee immediately.
2. **Explicit retention policy** — "Your files are deleted after 72 hours. We store the minimum necessary." Merchants uploading order records want to know where their data goes.
3. **Visible deadline tracking** — shows the product understands their actual stress.
4. **Category-specific output** — a merchant who picks "subscription cancelled" and gets a pack that's clearly built for that reason (not a generic template) will trust the product immediately.
5. **"Built for Stripe" signal** — Stripe merchants trust Stripe. Positioning as Stripe-native (not bank-agnostic) is a trust shortcut.
6. **Transparent pricing** — no hidden fees, no upsells at checkout, no "upgrade for the good checklist."

**Anti-trust signals to avoid:**
- "AI-powered" as a lead feature (triggers skepticism)
- "Guaranteed results" or "proven to work" (legally and ethically wrong)
- Dark patterns at checkout (upgrade nags, pre-selected add-ons)
- Testimonials that imply certainty of outcome

## 11. Anti-Commodity Messaging

The category that loses to commoditization: "AI rebuttal letter writer"
The category that wins: "dispute evidence assembly platform"

**Always frame as:**
- Evidence pack, not letter
- Category-specific, not generic
- Submission-ready, not draft
- For Stripe merchants, not for everyone

**Kill phrases (never use):**
- "AI-powered dispute helper"
- "Win your chargebacks"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Simple and easy" (this is high stakes, not casual)

**Power phrases (use consistently):**
- "Submission-grade"
- "Category-specific"
- "Built the way card networks want to see it"
- "30 minutes to a complete evidence pack"
- "Not a template — a structured assembly"
